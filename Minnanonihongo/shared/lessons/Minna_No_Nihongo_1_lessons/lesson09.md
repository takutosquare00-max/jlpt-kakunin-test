## II. Translation

### Sentence Patterns

1. I like music.
2. I can speak a little Japanese.
3. I often go to karaoke.

### Example Sentences

1. Do you like Japanese food?
   - ···Yes, I like it very much.

2. What sports do you like?
   - ···I like tennis.

3. Can you play the guitar?
   - ···Yes, I can play it a little.

4. Can you read kanji?
   - ···No, I can't read it at all.

5. How often do you go to concerts?
   - ···I go sometimes.

6. Why do you study Japanese?
   - ···Because I like Japan.

## IV. Grammar Explanation

### 1. Nが わかります／あります

The particle が marks the object of a small number of verbs and adjectives.

> ① わたしは 日本語が わかります。 — I understand Japanese.  
> ② わたしは 時間が あります。 — I have time.

---

### 2. Nが すきです／きらいです／じょうずです／へたです

The particle が is also used with な-adjectives すきな, きらいな, じょうずな, and へたな.

> ③ わたしは 音楽が 好きです。 — I like music.  
> ④ ミラーさんは テニスが 上手です。 — Mr. Miller is good at tennis.

[Note] The subject of the sentence marked with は is the person who likes/dislikes or is good/poor at something. The thing liked/disliked or at which one is good/poor is marked with が.

---

### 3. どんな N

To ask what kind of thing one likes, どんな N is used as the object of すきです, etc.

> ⑤ どんな 音楽が 好きですか。 — What kind of music do you like?  
> 　…クラシックが 好きです。 — ···I like classical.

---

### 4. よく／だいたい／たくさん／少し／あまり／全然

These adverbs indicate frequency or degree.

| 肯定 (affirmative) | 否定 (negative) |
|-------------------|-----------------|
| よく (often) | あまり〜ません (not much/often) |
| だいたい (mostly) | 全然〜ません (not at all) |
| たくさん (a lot) | 少し〜ません (not much) |
| 少し (a little) | |

> ⑥ よく 映画を 見ます。 — I often see movies.  
> ⑦ あまり 映画を 見ません。 — I don't see movies very often.  
> ⑧ 全然 映画を 見ません。 — I don't see movies at all.

---

### 5. S₁から、S₂

から at the end of a clause gives the reason for what is stated in S₂.

> ⑨ 日本が 好きだから、日本語を 勉強します。 — I study Japanese because I like Japan.

[Note] から follows the plain form of verbs and adjectives when explaining a reason informally; in polite sentences the ます/です form precedes から.

> ⑩ 明日 試験が ありますから、今日は 早く 帰ります。 — I'll go home early today because I have an exam tomorrow.

---

### 6. どうして

どうして is used to ask the reason for something.

> ⑪ どうして 日本語を 勉強しますか。 — Why do you study Japanese?  
> 　…日本が 好きだから です。 — ···Because I like Japan.
