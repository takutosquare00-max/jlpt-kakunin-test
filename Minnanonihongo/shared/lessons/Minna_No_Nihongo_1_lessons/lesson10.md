## II. Translation

### Sentence Patterns

1. There is a post box in front of the station.
2. The cat is under the table.
3. The department store is next to the bank.

### Example Sentences

1. Is there a post office near here?
   - ···Yes, there is. It's in front of the station.

2. Is there a computer in your room?
   - ···Yes, there is one.

3. Where is Yamada?
   - ···He's in the conference room.

4. Where is my bag?
   - ···It's under the table.

5. What is between the bank and the supermarket?
   - ···There's a bookshop.

6. Do you have chili sauce in this store?
   - ···I'm sorry, we're out of it at the moment.

### Conversation: **Do you have chili sauce in this store?**

| 話者 | 台詞 |
|------|------|
| Karina | Excuse me. Do you have chili sauce? |
| Clerk | Chili sauce? I'm afraid we're out of it at the moment. |
| Karina | I see. Is there a supermarket near here? |
| Clerk | Yes, there's one in front of the station. |
| Karina | Thank you. |

**Cultural Note:** A typical Japanese house has two types of rooms: Japanese-style rooms (和室 washitsu) with tatami mats, and Western-style rooms (洋室 yōshitsu) with wooden floors. The size of a room is measured by the number of tatami mats (畳 jō). One tatami is about 1.62 m². A 6-mat room is about 10 m² (roughly 100 sq. ft.).

## IV. Grammar Explanation

### 1. N が あります／います

あります is used for the existence of inanimate things (objects, plants). います is used for the existence of animate beings (people, animals).

> ① 駅の 前に ポストが あります。 — There is a post box in front of the station.  
> ② 公園に 子どもが います。 — There are children in the park.

---

### 2. N₁(place)に N₂が あります／います

に after a place noun indicates the location where something or someone exists.

> ③ 銀行の 前に 本屋が あります。 — There is a bookshop in front of the bank.  
> ④ 部屋に 山田さんが います。 — Mr. Yamada is in the room.

---

### 3. N₁は N₂(place)に あります／います

When the topic is the thing/person whose location is being stated, は is used to mark the topic, and に marks the location.

> ⑤ 郵便局は 駅の 前に あります。 — The post office is in front of the station.  
> ⑥ 山田さんは 会議室に います。 — Mr. Yamada is in the conference room.

---

### 4. N₁(thing/person/place)の N₂(position)

To describe the position of something relative to another object, the position word follows の after the reference object.

> ⑦ テレビは テーブルの 上に あります。 — The TV is on the table.  
> ⑧ 銀行は デパートの となりに あります。 — The bank is next to the department store.

---

### 5. N₁や N₂

や connects two or more nouns to give a non-exhaustive list (suggesting there are other items as well). It contrasts with と, which gives a complete list.

> ⑨ 机の 上に 本や ノートが あります。 — There are books, notebooks, and so on, on the desk.

---

### 6. 〜ですか (Rising intonation questions)

When a question word is not used but a rising intonation is intended, か at the end of a noun or adjective phrase can confirm whether something is the case.

> ⑩ チリソースは ありませんか。 — Don't you have chili sauce? / Do you have chili sauce?

This form with ませんか attached to あります is a polite inquiry, often used when asking a shop for a product.
